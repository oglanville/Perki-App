## 1. Voice in one line
Perki sounds like a sharp, trustworthy friend who quietly hands you money you forgot you had — calm, precise, and never salesy. _Confidence: High._

## 2. Voice attributes (the how)
1. **Trustworthy** — plain, honest, fintech-credible. Says what's true, never overpromises. _Do:_ "Read-only. We never move your money." _Don't:_ "Bank-level military-grade security!"
2. **Effortless** — short sentences, active voice, zero jargon. The reader never has to work. _Do:_ "Add a provider to see what you're owed." _Don't:_ "Initiate provider configuration to surface eligible benefit entitlements."
3. **Rewarding** — lands on the value, warmly, without hype. _Do:_ "Found £140 of Sky perks you forgot you had." _Don't:_ "UNLOCK INSANE SAVINGS NOW!!!"
4. **Quietly premium** — confident and understated. Calm beats loud. _Do:_ "The value you're already paying for — surfaced." _Don't:_ "The #1 revolutionary perks platform."
5. **Clear over clever** (inherited discipline) — if a line can be shorter, cut it. Plain words, no filler. _Confidence: High._

## 3. We are / We are not
| Perki IS | Perki is NOT |
|----------|--------------|
| Calm, precise, reassuring | Hyped, exclamatory, salesy |
| Plain-spoken and warm | Corporate, jargon-heavy |
| Confident and understated | Boastful or superlative-stuffed |
| A quiet concierge | A loud deal-hunter / coupon site |
| Honest about being read-only | Vague about money or security |
| Rewarding ("oh, nice") | Gimmicky or gamified |
_Confidence: High._

## 4. Tone by context (same voice, dial shifts)
| Context | Dial | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Landing / hero | Warm, bold, benefit-first | "£200+ a month in perks. You're using almost none." |
| How it works | Calm, reassuring, simple | "Read-only access. Perki never moves your money." |
| Value moment / success | Quietly delighted | "Nice — that's £7 a week back in your pocket." |
| Empty state | Encouraging, light | "Add a provider to uncover your hidden perks." |
| Error | Plain, helpful, blameless | "That email looks off — mind checking it?" |
| Reminders / notifications | Useful, low-key, never naggy | "Your Greggs perk resets Sunday — grab it." |
| Pitch deck / investor | Confident, commercial, precise | "The only cross-provider personal perk inventory." |
| Support | Human, patient, direct | "Happy to help — here's what's happening." |
_Confidence: High for product surfaces; Medium for support (no support corpus yet)._

## 5. Vocabulary
**Use:** surface, perks, value, already paying for, hidden, your plan/tier, claim, reset, read-only, unlock (sparingly), worth.
**Avoid:** deals, coupons, discounts-as-hero (Perki isn't a coupon site), savings hacks, "revolutionary/game-changing/best-in-class", "synergy", "leverage", exclamation spam, fake urgency ("act now"), emoji as decoration.
_Confidence: High._

## 6. Mechanics
- **British English** (surfaces, colour, optimise). _Confidence: High._
- **Sentence case** everywhere — headings, buttons, labels. Never Title Case or ALL CAPS.
- **Short sentences.** One idea each. Cut filler words ("just", "simply", "actually").
- **Active voice**, second person ("you/your").
- **Numbers earn their place** — concrete value beats vague ("£140", not "loads").
- **One exclamation mark max** per screen, ideally none.
- **Buttons are verbs that act:** "See my perks", "Claim it", "Mark as used" — not "Submit"/"OK".
- **Errors help, never blame:** name the problem + the fix, kindly.

## 7. Worked rewrites
- ❌ "Maximise your subscription ROI with our cutting-edge perk optimisation engine!"
  ✅ "See every perk you're already paying for — in one place."
- ❌ "Error: invalid input."

## 9. Rules (signed off 2026-06-03) 🔒
1. **Humour** — light wit, **never jokey**. Warmth over gags. A dry, knowing smile, not a punchline.
2. **"You" vs "we"** — **second person by default** ("your perks"); use "we" only for trust statements ("we never move your money").
3. **Emoji** — **none**. Not in product UI, not in copy. (Reinforces the premium, design-system rule.)
4. **One voice** — a single Perki voice everywhere; **slightly dial up** "confident / commercial / precise" for the pitch deck and investor materials. No separate consumer vs investor voice.
