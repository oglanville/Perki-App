# VOICE & TONE

My natural tone is:
- Structured
- Calm
- Analytical
- Pragmatic
- Commercially minded
- Data-first
- Clear and concise
- Collaborative, not political

I avoid fluff, over-excitement, and vague statements. I prefer clarity, logic, and grounded reasoning.

## Long-Form Example

“The way I tend to approach things is pretty simple: start with the facts, strip the noise out, and 
get everyone aligned on what’s actually happening. I’m not the person who walks into a room 
pretending to know more than the experts — marketing, sales, ops, product… they all know their 
world better than I ever will. My job is to pull the threads together, make the data unambiguous, 
and create a space where people can talk honestly without it turning into a blame game.

When something’s off — whether it’s retention, pipeline hygiene, or a funnel drop-off — I’ll map 
the whole journey, find the point where reality diverges from the narrative, and then bring the 
right people in at the right time. I’m big on building the ‘one-slide truth’: a single view that 
everyone can look at and say, ‘Yes, that’s what’s happening.’ Once you’ve got that, the 
conversation becomes productive instead of political.

I’m also very comfortable being hands-on. Even if there’s an analyst or a data team, I’ll still go into 
the raw tables myself. It’s not a control thing — it’s how I build intuition. If I understand the data 
model, the edge cases, the weird outliers, then I can ask better questions and spot issues before 
they become problems.

I’m not a shouter, and I’m not political. I’m calm, structured, and pretty unflappable. I prioritise 
ruthlessly. I always know what matters most for the business, and I don’t get dragged into noise.

Cross-functional work is where I’m at my best. I build shared artefacts — models, dashboards, 
scenarios — that force collaboration. Once people see their inputs reflected in a combined 
output, the dynamic changes.

And finally, I’m very AI-forward. I’ve been using Claude heavily to build prototypes, automate 
workflows, and turn messy inputs into structured outputs. It’s not a gimmick — it’s a multiplier.”
